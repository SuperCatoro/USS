About Pyrate (Co-Founder & Co-Chair, DTC)
Pyrate Ruby Passell is a multidisciplinary maker and governance technologist building infrastructure for upstream AI safety. She co-develops the technical and policy architecture of the Upstream Safety System (USS) and the Inclusive Lifelong Multistakeholder Model (ILMM), including classifier experimentation, deterministic governance layers, and repository development.
As Co-Founder and Co-Chair of the Dynamic Teen Coalition (DTC) — the first and only teen board within United Nations processes — Py contributes to frameworks that center teen voices in global digital governance. She serves as a Mentor for the ITU's First Citiverse Challenge, is a CERN Open Quantum Institute (OQI) Friend, and is the first Under 18 Changemaker for UN Partnerships at Our Future Agenda.
Py's technical foundation spans hands-on engineering and creative production. She won the 2018 Australian Youth Rocketry Challenge, builds mechatronics and combat robotics systems, and produces educational media that integrates human-centric storytelling with AI-assisted workflows. Her work reflects the combination of maker ethics, systems thinking, and emerging leadership in digital-governance ecosystems.
